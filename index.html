Step-by-Step Guide to Managing Your GEPCO Bill Online

Wondering how to begin managing your GEPCO bill online? The process is simple. Follow these easy steps to get started:

Step 1: Visit the Official GEPCO Bill Portal

The first step is to navigate to the GEPCO bill portal. Once you're on the homepage, you’ll see a section where you can input your billing details to access your latest bill.

Step 2: Input Your Reference Number or Consumer ID

To view your GEPCO bill, you will need either your 14-digit reference number or your 10-digit Consumer ID, both of which are listed on your previous bill. Enter this information carefully to avoid any errors.

Step 3: View Your Bill

Once you've entered the correct information, your bill will appear on the screen. You can review the total amount, the due date, and any additional charges that may apply for late payments. Take a moment to verify the information before proceeding to the payment stage.

Step 4: Pay Your Bill Online

After reviewing your bill, you can pay it directly through various online payment methods. GEPCO provides multiple options, including bank transfers, mobile wallets (JazzCash, Easypaisa), and credit/debit cards.

Step 5: Download or Print Your Bill

If you prefer to keep a record of your bill for future reference, you can download it as a PDF or print it directly from the portal. This ensures you have a hard copy for your records, should you need it later.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) About GEPCO Bill Online

Still have questions about how to manage your GEPCO bill online? Check out our FAQ section below:

  • Q: Can I access my old bills?
    A: Yes, the GEPCO online portal allows you to access your bill history for up to six months.
  • Q: What if I enter the wrong reference number?
    A: Double-check the number and re-enter it. The system will prompt you if the reference number or Consumer ID is invalid.
  • Q: Is it safe to pay my bill online?
    A: Yes, GEPCO uses secure payment gateways to ensure your personal and financial information is protected.
